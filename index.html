The Project

This project was developed as part of the ICT4D Crash Course 2026. It aims to demonstrate the power of Open Data and Geospatial analysis in addressing global environmental challenges. By visualizing historical trends and local risks, we provide a foundation for data-driven resilience planning.

Core Goals

  • Visibility: Centralizing 35 years of global flood records.
  • Precision: Identifying infrastructure at risk within a 1km buffer.
  • Accessibility: Providing open tools for custom dataset mapping.

Research & Methodology

The analytical pipeline used to transform raw data into intelligence.

01

Extraction

Automated extraction of 35 years of flood records from the RIMES/DFO Archive and infrastructure coordinates via Overpass API (OSM).

02

Spatial Processing

Application of the Haversine formula to calculate spherical distances between point-based flood events and polygonal health facilities.

03

Risk Filtering

Classification of "At-Risk" facilities based on a defined 1,000m proximity threshold to historical flood centers.
